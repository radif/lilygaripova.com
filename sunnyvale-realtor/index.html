Why Sunnyvale

Sunnyvale sits at the heart of Silicon Valley, between Mountain View (west), Cupertino (south), and Santa Clara (east). The school district situation is unusual: most of Sunnyvale's elementary students attend Sunnyvale School District (K-8) but the high schools come from one of two districts depending on address, Fremont Union HSD (Cupertino + west Sunnyvale, including Homestead High and Fremont High) or Sunnyvale's small slice of Santa Clara Unified for the east side. The Cupertino Union side carries the well-known California school premium.

Lily has 2 documented Sunnyvale closings including the recent $3.3M Cheshire Way closing (May 2026). The neighborhood split: north Sunnyvale (closer to NASA Ames / Moffett Field, mid-tier schools, $1.4M-$2M); south Sunnyvale (closer to Cupertino, top-tier schools, $1.8M-$3M+); downtown / Murphy Street area (walkable, mixed-use, $900K-$1.5M condos and smaller SFH). Cherry Chase + the cluster of streets near Cupertino Union's top elementaries carry $500K-$1M premiums over comparable Sunnyvale housing stock outside that boundary.

Schools (Sunnyvale + Fremont Union HSD)

Sunnyvale's school assignment is the textbook Santa Clara County two-district pattern: three K-8 districts plus one high school district, with the K-8 boundary determining everything below 9th grade and the Fremont Union HSD boundary determining high school.

  • Sunnyvale School District (K-8) covers most central and west Sunnyvale addresses.
  • Cupertino Union School District (K-8) covers south Sunnyvale tracts near the Cupertino border. CUSD is one of the strongest K-8 districts in California; the Sunnyvale addresses inside its boundary carry a noticeable price premium.
  • Santa Clara Unified covers east-side Sunnyvale tracts near the Santa Clara border (K-12 single-district).
  • Fremont Union High School District (FUHSD, 9-12, 9,000+ students) runs every comprehensive high school for Sunnyvale and Cupertino: Cupertino High (U.S. News #29 California), Homestead High (#65), Fremont High, Monta Vista High and Lynbrook High (both in Cupertino city but FUHSD assignment lines reach into Sunnyvale).

The high school you are zoned for is the dominant school-related driver of Sunnyvale property prices. Cupertino High and Homestead High families pay more per square foot than Fremont High families for comparable single-family stock; the FUHSD boundary lines do not match the city limits, so the same Sunnyvale ZIP can include three different high school assignments depending on the exact address. Verifying the FUHSD high school for the specific street before offering is the most consequential school-related action a Sunnyvale buyer can take.

Sunnyvale FAQ

What are Sunnyvale price ranges in 2026?

Single-family in south Sunnyvale (Cupertino Union schools, top-tier attendance) typically runs $1.8M-$3M+. Cherry Chase and the Homestead-High-feed pockets push higher. North Sunnyvale (mid-tier schools) runs $1.4M-$2M. Downtown / Murphy Street condos and smaller SFH run $900K-$1.5M. The May 2026 Cheshire Way closing at $3.3M is representative of the south-Sunnyvale single-family tier.

School district situation in Sunnyvale, what should I know?

Sunnyvale's K-8 district is Sunnyvale School District for most addresses; some southern addresses are in Cupertino Union School District (the top-tier K-8 district that feeds Homestead High). High school is Fremont Union HSD (Homestead High, Fremont High) for most addresses; a sliver on the east side is Santa Clara Unified. The Cupertino Union K-8 boundary is the single biggest price-determinant within the city; verify exact attendance by address.

Sunnyvale vs Mountain View vs Cupertino?

Cupertino is the most expensive of the three (Apple HQ + the strongest Cupertino Union school attendance). Mountain View is closer to Google's main campuses and downtown character. Sunnyvale is the largest of the three by population, has the widest inventory variety, and the most-spread price range, pockets in the Cupertino-feed area approach Cupertino prices, while north Sunnyvale near NASA Ames runs ~30% lower for comparable inventory.

What's the commute math from Sunnyvale to the major tech employers?

Apple Park (Cupertino): 10-20 minutes south on De Anza Blvd / 280. Google (Mountain View): 10-15 minutes west on 101 / Central Expressway. LinkedIn (Sunnyvale): in-city, 5-15 minutes. Yahoo / Verizon Sunnyvale: in-city. NVIDIA (Santa Clara): 10-15 minutes east. Sunnyvale's central Silicon Valley position is the structural commute advantage.
