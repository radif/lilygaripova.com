Why Pacifica

Pacifica sits on the Pacific Coast immediately south of Daly City, separated from the rest of the Peninsula by Sweeney Ridge and accessible via Hwy 1 (the coastal route) or Hwy 280 / 92 (the inland route). Population around 38,000. The city is structurally a coastal chain of beach communities: Sharp Park / Vallemar (north), Rockaway Beach / Pedro Point (central), and Linda Mar (south). Pacifica School District handles K-8 (2,652 students, state rank top 20%, average testing 9 out of 10). Jefferson Union HSD (headquartered in Daly City) handles 9-12 via Terra Nova HS (U.S. News #698 California) and Oceana HS (U.S. News #702 California). Buyers concerned about HS rankings often consider Sequoia Union HSD addresses just to the south for the Carlmont / Menlo-Atherton attendance.

Schools (Pacifica SD + Jefferson Union HSD)

Pacifica School District (K-8) serves 2,652 students; PublicSchoolReview state rank #344 of 1,907 (top 20%)average testing 9 out of 10. Jefferson Union High School District (JUHSD, 9-12, headquartered in Daly City) operates Terra Nova HS (U.S. News #698 California) and Oceana HS (#702 California) as the two Pacifica high schools; district also includes Jefferson HS and Westmoor HS in Daly City.

K-8 quality is solid mid-tier; 9-12 attainment drops sharply at JUHSD level. Buyers concerned about HS rankings often consider Sequoia Union HSD addresses just to the south (Carlmont, Menlo-Atherton attendance) for the academic step-up, at a significant pricing premium. The Pacifica K-8 to JUHSD 9-12 transition is a noticeable academic step-down compared to the K-8 strength; this is the single biggest school-related variable Pacifica family buyers should weigh.

Hospitals and birthing centers

Critical caveat for Pacifica family buyers: Pacifica has NO in-city hospital with labor and delivery, and the formerly-default North County option closed. Seton Medical Center in Daly City closed its L&D service in 2015. Pacifica families now have no L&D facility in the immediate north county; PPO families drive over Sweeney Ridge to Mills-Peninsula Burlingame (~20-30 min via 280 / 92) or into San Francisco (~25-40 min). Kaiser members go to Kaiser Redwood City (~25-35 min) or Kaiser SF (~30-40 min). Family buyers should know that ALL labor and delivery requires driving out of the city; this is a real lifestyle factor for expecting families.

Birthing centers: what matters

Pacifica is one of the most underserved Bay Area cities for in-corridor L&D. Plan for a 20 to 40 minute drive in any direction for any labor and delivery scenario. Build this into prenatal-care scheduling, particularly for the final weeks of pregnancy. Coastal Hwy 1 closures (occasional landslide closures, especially north of Devil's Slide tunnel) can extend the drive time; the 280 / 92 inland route is the more reliable option in any weather scenario.

For PPO family buyersMills-Peninsula Burlingame is the closest Peninsula option (Sutter Family Birth Center, Level II NICU). For high-acuity (Level IV NICU), UCSF Mission Bay in San Francisco is the regional destination.

For Kaiser membersKaiser Redwood City and Kaiser San Francisco are the two closest Kaiser L&D options. Kaiser Redwood City handles routine delivery above 34 weeks; premature below 34 weeks transfers to Kaiser Santa Clara or Kaiser SF NICU.

Hospital network coverage depends on your insurance plan. Lily does not advise on medical coverage decisions; for in-network confirmation contact your insurer directly. Crime, hazards, and ratings

Pacifica stacks the structural hazards that define the San Mateo coast: the San Andreas Fault comes ashore at Mussel Rock roughly two miles east, the coastal bluffs face active erosion driving managed-retreat actions at the Esplanade and Pacifica State Beach, and the eastern slopes carry Very High and Moderate to High fire hazard. Inland flat tracts behind the beaches carry Moderate liquefaction; landslide risk dominates on the hillsides.

Pacifica FAQ

What are Pacifica price ranges in 2026?

Pacifica single-family typically runs $900K to $1.5M for inland tracts (Sharp Park, Park Pacifica), $1.1M to $1.8M for the coastal hillside view tracts (Pedro Point, Linda Mar hillside), and $1.3M to $2M+ for the premium ocean-front and ocean-view Linda Mar coastal tracts. Condos and townhomes run $600K to $1M. The February 2026 Andover Dr closing at $1.103M is representative of the inland to coastal-hillside transition tier.

What's the deal with Pacifica hospitals?

Pacifica is one of the most hospital-underserved cities in the Bay Area. There is no in-city hospital with labor and delivery, and Seton Medical Center in Daly City (the formerly-default North County option) closed its L&D service in 2015. All labor and delivery requires driving 20 to 40 minutes off-coast: Mills-Peninsula Burlingame (~20-30 min via 280 / 92) for Sutter PPO, UCSF Mission Bay or CPMC in San Francisco (~25-40 min) for higher-acuity, Kaiser Redwood City or Kaiser SF for Kaiser members. For family-planning buyers this is a real consideration, build the drive into prenatal-care scheduling and plan for the late-pregnancy contingency.

Coastal living, what should I know?

Pacifica's coastal exposure is the lifestyle attraction and the structural cost. The salt air and fog accelerate the wear cycle on exterior paint, wood trim, window seals, and metal fixtures, plan for a more frequent maintenance cycle than inland properties. Coastal bluff and beach-front properties carry erosion risk that requires specialized geotech disclosure review; Esplanade Drive in particular has had documented bluff-erosion property loss in the last 15 years. What is Linda Mar and why is it the most popular Pacifica neighbourhood?

Linda Mar is the south Pacifica neighbourhood centered on Linda Mar Boulevard, Crespi Drive, and the Linda Mar Beach valley between Sweeney Ridge and Montara Mountain. It is Pacifica's largest neighbourhood and the surf and family-buyer center, with Linda Mar State Beach, Taco Bell on the beach (a Pacifica landmark), and the Linda Mar Shopping Center. Single-family is $1.1M to $1.8M for inland tracts (1950s and 1960s Doelger tract), $1.5M to $2.2M for hillside view tracts, and $1.8M to $3M+ for the ocean-view bluff homes along Linda Mar Beach. Feeders are Cabrillo or Ocean Shore Elementary, Ingrid B. Lacy Middle, and Oceana High.

What is the Esplanade Drive bluff-erosion situation?

Esplanade Drive sits on the Sharp Park coastal bluff above Pacifica State Beach. Documented bluff erosion over the last 15 years has caused condo and apartment building condemnations, including the 2016 to 2017 yellow-tagging and demolition of multiple oceanfront buildings, and the 2024 partial loss of additional structures. The California Coastal Commission has classified portions of Esplanade as a managed-retreat zone. Lily walks every Esplanade-area listing with the geotech and Coastal Commission documentation in mind; coastal bluff properties anywhere in Pacifica require specialized engineer review before offer.

What is the Hwy 1 commute and the inland-route alternative?

Pacifica's only direct access to Hwy 1 runs through the Devil's Slide tunnel (south to Half Moon Bay) and north through Daly City to Hwy 101 or I-280. Pacifica to downtown San Francisco is 25 to 40 minutes via Hwy 1 to I-280, depending on traffic and weather. Atmospheric-river landslide closures of Hwy 1 north of Devil's Slide tunnel have happened multiple times in the last 10 years; the inland 280 / 92 route via San Bruno is the reliable alternative in any weather scenario and is the recommended route for hospital trips and time-sensitive commutes.
