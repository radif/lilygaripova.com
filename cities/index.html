Livermore, CA

East-end Tri-Valley city. Wine country lifestyle (Livermore Valley AVA, 50+ wineries 15-25 min east). LLNL / Sandia employer cluster. 15-25% price discount to Pleasanton for comparable inventory; no BART (terminus is Dublin/Pleasanton, 10-15 min drive).

Sunnyvale, CA

San Jose, CA

Largest Bay Area city, widest inventory variety. Almaden Valley + Cambrian (top schools), Willow Glen (downtown character), Rose Garden / Naglee Park (established prestige), Berryessa / Evergreen (mid-tier). 13+ school districts fragment the city.

Open the San Jose guide → Silicon Valley · Santa Clara County

Los Gatos, CA

South Bay foothills between Saratoga and Campbell. Los Gatos-Saratoga JUHSD (state #27 of 1,907, top 5%; Los Gatos High #89 California). Walkable Santa Cruz Avenue downtown, hillside Aldercroft / Black Road estate inventory, El Camino Hospital Los Gatos in-city L&D (Level II NICU).

3 documented Los Gatos closings · $4.02M local volume · LGSUHSD (state #27, top 5% California)

Open the Los Gatos guide → Silicon Valley · Santa Clara County

Campbell, CA

Central South Bay surrounded by San Jose. Campbell Union HSD (5 high schools ranging Leigh #83 California to Del Mar #574, the school assignment drives a $200K-$500K price spread). Walkable downtown Campbell Avenue, Campbell Farmers Market regional draw.

Open the Campbell guide → Silicon Valley · Santa Clara County

Mountain View, CA

Open the Mountain View guide → Silicon Valley · Santa Clara County

Palo Alto, CA

Stanford-anchored Peninsula city. Palo Alto Unified School District (top 1% California, Paly + Gunn high schools). Stanford Hospital + Lucile Packard Level IV NICU. University Avenue + California Avenue walkable downtowns, Crescent Park / Old Palo Alto / Professorville historic districts.

0 documented Palo Alto closings yet · broader Peninsula coverage via Mountain View / San Mateo / East Palo Alto record

Open the Palo Alto guide → Silicon Valley · San Mateo County

Menlo Park, CA

Meta-anchored Peninsula city. Three K-8 districts (Menlo Park City, Las Lomitas, Ravenswood pockets) + Sequoia Union HSD (Menlo-Atherton High). Sand Hill Road venture cluster, Stanford-adjacent. West-Menlo $3M+ vs East-Menlo $1.5M-$2.5M.

0 documented Menlo Park closings yet · broader Peninsula coverage via Mountain View / San Mateo / Belmont / East Palo Alto record

Open the Menlo Park guide → Peninsula · San Mateo County

Redwood City, CA

Central Peninsula city with three K-8 districts (Redwood City SD, Belmont-Redwood Shores SD, Woodside Elementary) + Sequoia Union HSD. Downtown Caltrain corridor revitalization, Stanford in Redwood City, Mid-Peninsula price advantage to Atherton / Menlo Park.

0 documented Redwood City closings yet · broader Peninsula coverage via Belmont / San Mateo / Foster City record

Open the Redwood City guide → Peninsula · San Mateo County

Belmont, CA

Mid-Peninsula hillside city between San Mateo and Redwood City. Belmont-Redwood Shores SD (K-8, top California) + Sequoia Union HSD (Carlmont High top 100 California). Hillside views, walkable Old County Road, family-buyer Peninsula entry point.

1 documented Belmont closing · $1.82M local volume · BRSSD + Sequoia Union HSD (Carlmont)

Open the Belmont guide → Peninsula · San Mateo County

San Mateo, CA

Largest Peninsula city by population. San Mateo-Foster City SD (K-8) + San Mateo Union HSD (5 high schools, Aragon + San Mateo + Hillsdale + Burlingame + Capuchino, spanning a wide rank range). Downtown B Street walkable, Hillsdale Caltrain, Bay Meadows master-planned redevelopment.

1 documented San Mateo closing · $1.29M local volume · San Mateo-Foster City SD + SMUHSD

Open the San Mateo guide → Peninsula · San Mateo County

Foster City, CA

Master-planned waterfront city on a former salt-marsh island. San Mateo-Foster City SD + San Mateo Union HSD (San Mateo High + Aragon). Hwy 92 single-bridge dependency, lagoon-canal residential pattern, Genentech-Gilead biotech employer corridor.

1 documented Foster City closing · $1.30M local volume (Perseus Ln, May 2026) · San Mateo-Foster City SD

Open the Foster City guide → Silicon Valley · Santa Clara County

Santa Clara, CA

Single-district city: Santa Clara Unified School District (K-12, also covers parts of Sunnyvale). Wilcox High + Santa Clara High + Adrian Wilcox + New Valley. Levi's Stadium, NVIDIA + Intel + AMD employer corridor, Mission College, Old Quad historic district.

1 documented Santa Clara closing · $1.65M local volume · Santa Clara Unified School District

Open the Santa Clara guide → Silicon Valley · Santa Clara County

Saratoga, CA

Foothill premium city west of Cupertino. Los Gatos-Saratoga JUHSD (Saratoga High #25 California). K-8 fragmented across 4 districts (Saratoga Union, Cupertino Union, Campbell Union, Moreland); the Cupertino Union pocket feeds Lynbrook / Monta Vista (Fremont Union HSD) not Saratoga High. Hakone Gardens, historic Saratoga Village.

1 documented Saratoga closing · $0.80M local volume · LGSUHSD (Saratoga High #25 California)

Open the Saratoga guide → City + County · San Francisco County

San Francisco, CA

Single city-county. SFUSD uses choice-enrollment (address does NOT guarantee school assignment, unique among Bay Area cities). Most diverse housing stock: Victorians in Noe Valley / Pacific Heights / Western Addition, mid-century in Sunset / Richmond, condos in SOMA / Mission Bay / Hayes Valley. UCSF Mission Bay Level IV NICU, ZSFG Level I trauma.

4 documented San Francisco closings · $4.97M local volume · SFUSD (choice enrollment) + UCSF + ZSFG

Open the San Francisco guide → Peninsula · San Mateo County

East Palo Alto, CA

Distinct from Palo Alto despite the name. Ravenswood City Elementary SD (K-8, challenged) + Sequoia Union HSD (high schoolers go to Menlo-Atherton or East Palo Alto Academy charter). Inside Lucile Packard Stanford Level IV NICU catchment despite K-8 district struggles. Gentrification arc since Facebook moved next door.

1 documented East Palo Alto closing · $0.91M local volume · Ravenswood SD + Sequoia Union HSD

Open the East Palo Alto guide → Pacific Coast · San Mateo County

Pacifica, CA

Pacific coastal city, distinct microclimate (fog + cooler). Pacifica SD (K-8) + Jefferson Union HSD (Terra Nova High, Oceana High). Critical hospital caveat: Seton Daly City closed L&D in 2015; Pacifica families drive 20-40 min over Sweeney Ridge for L&D. Surf town character, Devils Slide.

1 documented Pacifica closing · $1.10M local volume (Feb 2026) · Pacifica SD + Jefferson Union HSD

Open the Pacifica guide → East Contra Costa · Contra Costa County

Brentwood, CA

East Contra Costa agricultural-turned-suburban city. Brentwood Union ESD (K-8) + Liberty Union HSD (Liberty High, Heritage High). Affordable Bay Area family-buyer entry point, large lots, master-planned tracts. Antioch BART terminus 15 min west, then 50 min to SF.

2 documented Brentwood closings · $1.87M local volume · BUESD + Liberty Union HSD

Open the Brentwood guide → Central Contra Costa · Contra Costa County

Martinez, CA

Contra Costa County seat. Martinez USD (K-12, single district). Historic downtown along Main Street, Joe DiMaggio birthplace, the Carquinez Strait waterfront, Martinez Refinery (Shell). Affordable Contra Costa alternative to Walnut Creek with John Muir Walnut Creek 10-15 min away.

1 documented Martinez closing · $1.05M local volume · Martinez USD + John Muir Walnut Creek catchment

Open the Martinez guide → East Contra Costa · Contra Costa County

Clayton, CA

Small foothill city at the base of Mt. Diablo State Park, east of Concord. Mt. Diablo Unified School District (same as Concord, includes Clayton Valley Charter from 2012 conversion). Estate-character single-family on larger lots, hiking + Mt. Diablo trailhead access, less density than neighbouring Concord.

1 documented Clayton closing · $0.75M local volume · MDUSD + Clayton Valley Charter

Open the Clayton guide → North Bay · Solano County

Vallejo, CA

Solano County's largest city, on the Carquinez Strait north of the Bay Bridge. Vallejo City USD (K-12). Critical hospital caveat: Sutter Solano closed L&D in May 2021; Kaiser Vallejo is the only in-city L&D option, PPO families drive 20-30 min to Queen of the Valley (Napa) or NorthBay Fairfield. Affordable Bay Area entry, ferry access to SF.

1 documented Vallejo closing · $0.32M local volume (career-low entry-point price) · Vallejo City USD + Kaiser Vallejo

Open the Vallejo guide → East Bay · Alameda County

Newark, CA

Tri-City corner between Fremont and Union City. Newark Unified School District, Bridgeway / Cedar Boulevard master-planned tracts, I-880 + Dumbarton Bridge commute corridor, 10-15% price discount to Fremont for comparable single-family.

1 documented Newark closing · $1.30M local volume · Newark Unified School District

Open the Newark guide → 680 Corridor · Contra Costa County

Concord, CA

Largest city in Contra Costa County. West Concord (Walnut Creek-adjacent) vs East Concord (Clayton-adjacent). 15-30% price discount to Walnut Creek for comparable inventory. Two BART stations, Mt. Diablo Unified schools.

6 documented Concord closings · ~$5.4M local volume · Mt. Diablo Unified School District

Open the Concord guide → East Bay · Alameda County

Hayward, CA

Second-largest Alameda County city after Oakland. Widest inventory variety, lowest entry price in I-880/I-580 corridor while still offering BART. Hayward Hills + Five Canyons premium hillside vs central walkable BART corridor vs south/Mt. Eden mid-tier.

4 documented Hayward closings · ~$3.2M local volume · 3 BART stations

Open the Hayward guide → East Bay · Alameda County

Union City, CA

Between Hayward and Fremont on I-880. Multi-generational Filipino-American and Asian-American buyer pools. New Haven Unified (James Logan High). Single BART station, walkshed premium matters. Priced between Hayward (lower) and Fremont (higher).

5 documented Union City closings · ~$4.9M local volume · New Haven Unified School District

Open the Union City guide → East Bay · Alameda County

Oakland, CA

Most neighborhood-dependent Bay Area market. Rockridge / Temescal / Piedmont Avenue premium ($1.3M-$2M+); Montclair / Crocker Highlands hills ($1.5M-$3M+); flats $700K-$1.2M. Oakland Unified varies dramatically by attendance area. Verify before any offer.

1 documented Oakland closing · broader East Bay coverage via Fremont / Hayward / Castro Valley / Union City record

Open the Oakland guide → East Bay · Alameda Island

Alameda, CA

Distinctive Bay Area island community. Alameda Unified School District (top 20% California, Alameda High #166 + Encinal #270), East End Victorians vs Bay Farm Island mid-century vs Alameda Point master-planned. Alameda Hospital has no L&D (off-island travel required).

1 documented Alameda closing · $0.60M local volume · Alameda Unified School District

Open the Alameda guide → 680 Corridor · Contra Costa County

Pleasant Hill, CA

Central Contra Costa between Walnut Creek and Concord. Mt. Diablo Unified (College Park High flagship, top 250 California), Acalanes UHSD edge cases on the north slope. Pleasant Hill BART + immediate access to John Muir Walnut Creek Level III NICU.

1 documented Pleasant Hill closing · $1.15M local volume · MDUSD + Acalanes UHSD edge

Open the Pleasant Hill guide → North Bay · Solano County

Vacaville, CA

Mid-Solano city between Fairfield and Davis on I-80. Vacaville USD (most addresses) + Travis USD (pockets near Travis Air Force Base). NorthBay VacaValley Hospital (in-city) + Kaiser Vacaville. Affordable family-buyer entry, large-lot newer construction, agricultural roots (Nut Tree).

1 documented Vacaville closing · $0.42M local volume · Vacaville USD + NorthBay VacaValley

Open the Vacaville guide → East Contra Costa · Contra Costa County

Antioch, CA

Largest East Contra Costa city. Antioch Unified School District (one district, several high schools spanning a wide rank range). Antioch BART terminus (the easternmost BART station), Sutter Delta Medical Center in-city L&D, Kaiser Antioch. Most affordable per-square-foot in the Contra Costa cluster.

2 documented Antioch closings · $1.41M local volume · Antioch USD + in-city L&D

Open the Antioch guide → Tri-Valley · Contra Costa County

Danville, CA

Tri-Valley corridor between San Ramon and Alamo. San Ramon Valley Unified (top 5% California), Monte Vista High flagship (#93 California), walkable downtown Hartz Avenue, hillside / Blackhawk-adjacent estate inventory.
