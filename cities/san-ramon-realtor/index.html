Why San Ramon

San Ramon sits at the north end of the Tri-Valley, between Dublin (south) and Danville (north). The school district is San Ramon Valley Unified, one of the top-ranked districts in California. Bishop Ranch is the major employer cluster (Chevron, AT&T, PG&E, GE). The city splits between older central San Ramon (1970s-1990s housing) and the master-planned east side (Windemere, Gale Ranch, post-2000, with active Mello-Roos).

Schools (San Ramon Valley Unified School District)

San Ramon Valley Unified School District (SRVUSD) is one of the strongest large districts in California: 29,183 students across 37 schools, PublicSchoolReview state rank #53 of 1,907 California districts (top 5%). The district covers San Ramon, Danville, Alamo, and Blackhawk, and the strength is uniformly distributed: every high school in the district ranks in the top 5% of California, and there is no weak-feeder pocket the way some larger Bay Area districts have. The Dougherty Valley / north-San-Ramon side and the Twin Creeks / central-San-Ramon side both feed strong high schools, so the central question is which specific attendance area you buy into, not whether SRVUSD itself is strong.

Dougherty Valley High is the in-San-Ramon flagship. Dougherty Valley High School (~3,000 students, U.S. News #27 California, top 200 nationally, 90%+ AP participation, 4.1 average GPA) anchors the eastern Dougherty Valley master-planned tracts. California High School (~3,100 students, U.S. News #199 California) anchors the central-San-Ramon and Bishop Ranch corridor. San Ramon Valley High and Monte Vista High are the Danville flagships and pull buyers north across the city line for the boundary, but for in-San-Ramon buyers Dougherty Valley and California High are the central question. Windemere Ranch Middle and Iron Horse Middle are the top feeders.

San Ramon FAQ

What are San Ramon price ranges in 2026?

Single-family in older central San Ramon (1970s-1990s) typically runs $1.4M-$1.9M. Master-planned east San Ramon (Windemere, Gale Ranch) runs $1.6M-$2.5M plus active Mello-Roos $4K-$8K per year. Bishop Ranch / Crow Canyon corridor estate inventory pushes past $2.5M. Condos and townhomes city-wide run $700K-$1M.

San Ramon vs Dublin vs Pleasanton?

All three are Tri-Valley cities with strong schools and freeway access. San Ramon Valley Unified ranks above Dublin Unified and on par with Pleasanton Unified on most published measures. Pleasanton carries the school + downtown premium. Dublin has the most new-construction inventory + BART access. San Ramon has Bishop Ranch as the employer anchor + the largest concentration of newer master-planned with Mello-Roos.

Are Mello-Roos and HOA carrying costs higher in San Ramon than Dublin?

Often yes. Windemere and Gale Ranch carry some of the highest Mello-Roos rates in the Tri-Valley (newer bonds, longer remaining terms). Combined monthly carrying cost (HOA plus 1/12 of annual Mello-Roos) often runs $500-$1,000 per month on top of mortgage and property tax. Verify the exact CFD line items before any offer.

Bishop Ranch, who's there and what's the commute?

Bishop Ranch is the largest business park in northern California, anchored by Chevron, AT&T, PG&E, GE, and a long tail of smaller offices. Many San Ramon family buyers are Bishop Ranch employees commuting on-foot or via shuttle. For reverse-commute to South Bay (Apple / Google / NVIDIA), it's I-680 south to I-580 west to I-880 south, 50-80 minutes off-peak.

What does the Mello-Roos line cost on a typical Dougherty Valley home in 2026?

Dougherty Valley single-family homes (Windemere, Gale Ranch) typically carry annual Mello-Roos special tax in the $4,000 to $8,000 range, with some larger estate lots and newer phases running higher. The CFD line is in addition to the 1.0%-1.25% ad valorem property tax and any HOA dues, so the all-in carrying cost above mortgage and insurance is materially higher than central San Ramon. Bond amortization horizons vary by CFD; some run another 20-30 years. Always pull the parcel-specific CFD detail from the SRVUSD or the CFD administrator before offer.

How are San Ramon schools split between Dougherty Valley High and California High?

Dougherty Valley High School serves the master-planned eastern tracts (Windemere, Gale Ranch, the Dougherty Valley corridor) at about 3,000 students, ranked U.S. News #27 California. California High School serves central and southern San Ramon (Twin Creeks, the Bishop Ranch corridor, older 1970s-1990s tracts) at about 3,100 students, ranked U.S. News #199 California. Both are top-tier; Dougherty Valley is the newer flagship, California High the historic central flagship. Attendance is by exact parcel, verify with the SRVUSD registrar before any offer.
